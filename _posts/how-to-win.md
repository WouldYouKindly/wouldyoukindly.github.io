# Notes on How to Win Friends and Influence People

## Principle 1, never criticise

- Most people in most situations cannot accept criticism.
- It's not only useless, it will make them resent you.
- Or hate you for the rest of their life. Or yours.
- This doesn't mean you cannot access people's actions. You can do it in your mind or with other person who will keep it secret.
- Remember that you don't know the full story. Maybe there's a good explanation for what looks retarded from your POV.
- Notice and praise the good things they do.
- Don't talk about their mistakes. Don't criticise. Don't complain.

This conflicts with the need to let people know that their behaviour is bad for you. E.g., if someone always jokes about you being fat, you should be allowed to tell them to stop.


## Principle 2, give honest praise

- Make people feel that they are important
- On a daily basis - notice the small good things and compliment people

Interesting how he doesn't say whether people *should* be so dependant on the external validation - he simply states that giving it will make people like you more.

## Principle 3, make people want it

If you want a person to do something, make them want it.

- Give them reasons why doing that would be good FOR THEM
- Paint them an exciting picture of what they'll get if they do it
- Make them feel big and important (the opposite of what I'm doing when I'm trying to explain post-modernism)

I need to make girls want to go on a date with me.

- Profile should make them want me
- When we match, exchange 1-2 messages, then suggest a place, but mention that if they want to continue chatting that's cool too
- Thus I should have 3-4 nice places that I want to visit, or, ideally, have already vetted myself
